Getting Started

Claude Usage Tracker is a small floating panel that keeps your Claude subscription usage, live service availability, and (optionally) your API spend visible on your Mac — no need to open a browser and dig through account pages.

How it works

The app checks your Claude.ai account and status.claude.com on a timer (every 30 seconds by default) and shows the results in a compact panel: how much of your 5‑hour session and weekly limits you've used, and whether Claude is currently having any issues.

First steps

  1. 1
    Find the panel — it floats in the corner of your screen. If you don't see it, click the ◔ icon in the menu bar.
  2. 2
    Click the gear icon in the panel to open Settings.
  3. 3
    Go to the Claude.ai section and click Sign in to Claude.ai.
  4. 4
    Once connected, your Session and Weekly usage bars fill in automatically.
  5. 5
    Decide whether you want the panel always visible or tucked away behind the menu bar icon — see Appearance in Settings.
Your credentials stay on your Mac. The Claude.ai session key and any API key you add are stored in the macOS Keychain. The app talks directly to Anthropic's own servers to fetch your usage — nothing passes through a third party.
🖥

The Floating Panel

Session and Weekly usage

Each usage row shows two things at once: a bar for how much of your quota is used, and a thin vertical marker on the bar showing how far through the time window you currently are.

  • If the coloured fill is ahead of the marker, you're burning through that limit faster than the clock is moving.
  • If the fill is behind the marker, you're pacing comfortably.
  • The text underneath spells it out exactly, e.g. "⧗ 3h 31m elapsed · resets 2:59 PM".

The bar turns amber at 75% used and red at 90%, matching the thresholds used for notifications.

Availability

The coloured dot and label at the bottom of the panel (Operational / Degraded / Outage) reflect Claude's live system status. Click it to open status.claude.com in your browser for more detail.

Panel controls

🔄 Refresh
Fetches the latest usage and status right away, instead of waiting for the next scheduled check.
⚙️ Gear
Opens the Settings window.
⏻ Power
Quits Claude Usage Tracker.
Always on top switch
Also available directly in the panel's header — toggles whether the panel floats above every other window.

Moving the panel

Drag anywhere on the panel's background to reposition it. Its location is remembered the next time you open the app. If a display gets disconnected or rearranged and the saved spot is no longer on screen, the panel automatically moves itself back to the top-right of your main display.

Connecting Claude.ai

Claude Usage Tracker needs to be signed in to your Claude.ai account to read your Session and Weekly usage. This is a one-time setup.

Signing in

  1. 1
    Open Settings → Claude.ai and click Sign in to Claude.ai.
  2. 2
    A sign-in window opens. Use Continue with Google, or enter your email and the verification code Claude sends you.
  3. 3
    Once you land on your Claude.ai home page, the window closes automatically and your usage bars populate within a few seconds.

Manual session key (Advanced)

If the embedded sign-in doesn't work for you, the Claude.ai section also has an advanced field to paste a sessionKey cookie value directly.

Disconnecting

Click Remove next to your connection status in Settings → Claude.ai to sign out and clear the stored key.

When your session expires

If Claude.ai invalidates your session, the Session and Weekly rows will show "Not connected." Just sign in again the same way — nothing else needs to change.

Menu Bar Icon

What it shows

The menu bar icon displays your current Session usage as a compact percentage, e.g. ◔ 48%, so you can glance at it without opening the panel at all.

Showing and hiding the panel

How a click behaves depends on the Always on top setting:

  • Always on top ON — the panel stays permanently visible; clicking the icon just brings it to the front.
  • Always on top OFF — the panel is hidden until you need it. Left-click the icon to pop it up right underneath, and left-click again — or click anywhere outside the panel — to dismiss it.

Right-click menu

Right-click the icon for a quick menu with Settings… and Quit.

Whenever Always on top is off, the menu bar icon is kept on automatically — it's the only way to summon the panel, so the app won't let you hide it.
📈

Extra Tracking

Two additional rows can be switched on if you want more detail. Both are off by default and require nothing extra unless you enable them.

API console spend

Shows how much you've spent this month on the Anthropic API console. Go to Settings → API Console, turn on Track API spend, and paste in an Anthropic Admin API key (starts with sk-ant-admin…). The figure is refreshed roughly every two minutes, since billing data doesn't change second to second.

Fable weekly usage

Adds a Weekly (Fable) row showing your per-model weekly limit for Fable — the same figure shown in the Claude app's own usage area. Turn it on in Settings → Fable.

Settings

General

Refresh interval
A slider from 10 to 300 seconds (default 30s) controlling how often usage and status are checked.
Threshold notifications
Turns macOS notifications on or off for the 75% / 90% / 95% usage alerts — see Notifications below.
Launch at login
Starts Claude Usage Tracker automatically when you log in to your Mac.

Appearance

Always on top
Keeps the panel permanently visible above other windows, or hides it behind the menu bar icon — see Menu Bar Icon.
Panel opacity
A slider from 30% (see-through) to 100% (solid), applied instantly, so the panel can sit unobtrusively over whatever's behind it.
Show menu bar icon
Can be turned off only while Always on top is on — otherwise it's kept on so the panel always has a way to be reopened.
🔔

Notifications

When Threshold notifications is on (Settings → General), macOS notifies you as your Session or Weekly usage crosses 75%, 90%, and 95%.

How they behave

  • Each threshold fires only once per crossing — you won't be spammed while usage sits above 75%.
  • Once a limit resets (a new 5-hour session or a new week begins), the thresholds reset too, ready to fire again.
  • Session and Weekly limits are tracked independently, so crossing one doesn't affect the other.

FAQ

The panel disappeared and I can't find it.

If Always on top is off, the panel is hidden by design — click the ◔ icon in the menu bar to bring it back. If it was on and still seems to be missing, it may have been positioned off-screen after a display was disconnected or rearranged; the app automatically detects that and moves the panel back to the top-right of your main screen the next time it checks.

My usage bars say "Not connected."

Your Claude.ai session has most likely expired. Open Settings → Claude.ai and click Sign in to Claude.ai again — it only takes a few seconds.

Why is the API spend row blank, or slow to update?

Make sure Track API spend is switched on in Settings → API Console and that a valid Admin API key has been saved. Spend figures are also refreshed at most once every two minutes, even if your refresh interval is set lower, since billing data changes slowly.

Is my data safe?

Your Claude.ai session key and any API key are stored in the macOS Keychain, not in a plain file. The app connects directly to Anthropic's and Claude's own servers to fetch your usage — there's no analytics, telemetry, or third-party server involved.

Does this use an official Anthropic API?

Subscription usage and availability use the same internal endpoints the Claude.ai website itself relies on. They aren't officially documented, so an unannounced change on Anthropic's side could occasionally cause a reading to go blank until the app is updated. API spend, by contrast, uses Anthropic's official Cost Report API.

How do I quit the app?

Click the power icon in the panel's footer, or right-click the menu bar icon and choose Quit.

How do I get a newer version?

Download the latest .dmg from the project's GitHub Releases page (github.com/Cosmo-NZ/Floating-Claude-Usage-Tracker/releases) and drag it into Applications, replacing the previous version. Your Claude.ai connection and settings are preserved.
